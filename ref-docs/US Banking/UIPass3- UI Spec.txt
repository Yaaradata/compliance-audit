# UI Specification — MVP

Building on Pass 1 personas and Pass 2 capabilities + data model, this pass defines the screens, components, and interactions that turn the platform into a daily decision surface for each persona.

Three constraints shape every choice:

- **Decisions over data** — every screen opens with a signal, not a filter bar.
- **Visualization-led** — the primary canvas is always a graph, heatmap, cluster, or flow. Tables exist only as supporting drill detail.
- **Two-click root cause, structurally enforced** — any aggregate reaches an empirical record (`ControlInstance` / `StepExecution` / `Evidence`) within two clicks. Screens that fail this test do not ship.

---

## 1. UI Architecture

### 1.1 The three-zone canvas

Every persona surface follows the same layout shell:

- **Primary canvas** — the persona's central visualization (60–70% of viewport)
- **AI Insights rail** — right side (~320px), ranked, persona-tuned, dismissible
- **Action dock** — bottom, collapsible, queue of items requiring this user

### 1.2 Persistent platform chrome (top, ~56px)

Five elements visible on every screen:

- **Time scrubber** — global state-as-of date. Slides through history; entire view recomputes against event-sourced run-time entities.
- **Jurisdiction selector** — US / UK / IN / multi. Drives obligation lens.
- **Drill stack breadcrumb** — preserves context as the user drills (e.g., *Financial Crime → Sanctions Screening → Instance 2026-04-22 14:32*). Every node is back-navigable.
- **Natural-language search** — queries the entity graph ("show me failed callbacks on wires above $250K last month").
- **Persona switcher** — for users with multi-role access; respects SoD constraints.

### 1.3 Cross-cutting design rules

- **Same fact, different frames.** A `ControlInstance` rendered in the auditor's Workbench and the CRO's Cockpit is the *same record* with different surrounding visuals. Sharing a link preserves cross-persona drill.
- **Explainability is a first-class element**, not a tooltip. Every AI claim has a clickable "Explain this" affordance with confidence, source records, and counterfactual.
- **Time-travel is default state**, not a feature. Every screen answers "as of <date>" without re-modelling.
- **No raw RCM IDs in primary view** — IDs surface only in drill detail, lineage panels, and exports.

## 2. Screen Specifications

Three screens per persona — the MVP minimum to validate the ontology and prove the 2-click rule end-to-end.

### 2.1 CRO / CAO

#### Screen 1 — Risk Posture Cockpit
**Purpose.** Daily landing surface. Answers *"where am I exposed right now?"* on one screen, with one click to root cause. The CRO's defensible point of departure for any upward conversation.

**Layout.**
- **Posture banner (top, full width):** Enterprise Risk Exposure Score, 90-day sparkline, AI-generated one-line narrative ("*Exposure rising; driven by Model and Financial Crime*").
- **Risk domain heatmap (primary canvas):** 7 risk domains × last 13 weeks. Each cell = RES band, traffic-lit. Hover shows top contributing factors; click drills to that domain.
- **Appetite status strip (below heatmap):** 15 appetite tiles, horizontally scrollable. Each tile = current band + sparkline + threshold marker.
- **AI Insights rail:** top 3 emerging signals ranked by ΔRES contribution.
- **Action dock:** items the CRO must personally acknowledge (board attestations due, breaches requiring sign-off).

**Components.** Risk domain heatmap, posture banner, appetite tiles, insight cards, action chips, drill stack.

**Key interactions.**
- Click heatmap cell → domain panel showing top contributing controls (CES decomposition), open issues, KRI breaches.
- Click insight card → explainability panel: source `ControlInstance` / `KRIObservation` / `Issue` records, confidence, model version.
- Time scrubber → entire screen re-renders for historical date — including the AI narrative.

**2-click drill example.** Heatmap "Model" red cell → domain panel surfaces `KRI-MR-009` red + 17 open high-severity findings → click finding cluster → individual `Issue` records with linked `ControlInstance`s and remediation status.

---

#### Screen 2 — What Changed This Week
**Purpose.** Narrative-led weekly delta. Replaces the Monday morning pack with a generated, attributable story of what shifted and what action it requires.

**Layout.**
- **Generated narrative panel (top):** AI-authored 4–5 paragraphs. Every claim is a clickable anchor to source records. Confidence indicators inline.
- **Delta graph (middle):** stacked area showing RES contribution by domain over the week, with annotated events (issue raised, control failed, KRI crossed amber, new regulatory event).
- **Movers grid (below):** top 5 metrics that moved most. Each card = before/after, magnitude, plain-language driver, "investigate" CTA.
- **AI Insights rail:** anomalies (e.g., *"AML alert backlog acceleration outside 3-year seasonal pattern; 97% confidence"*).

**Components.** Narrative panel with anchored claims, annotated stacked area, mover cards, anomaly banner.

**Key interactions.**
- Click narrative anchor → opens explainability + lineage stack.
- Click annotation on delta graph → jumps to event source (issue, control instance, reg change).
- Click "investigate" on mover card → routes to relevant deep-dive screen.

---

#### Screen 3 — Emerging Risks Radar
**Purpose.** Surface leading indicators *before* they become breaches. The proactive cousin of the Cockpit. Designed around the foundational principle that the platform must compress time between activity drift and risk event.

**Layout.**
- **Radar (primary):** 2-axis scatter — likelihood (x) × velocity-of-change (y). Each point = candidate emerging risk; color = domain; size = potential exposure.
- **Signal source list (left rail):** which signals feed the radar (override-rate trends, exception-rate velocity, KRI direction, attrition signals, regulatory pipeline). Toggleable.
- **Watch list (right rail):** risks the CRO has flagged for monitoring; each shows trend since flagged.
- **Insight rail (deeper right):** ranked predicted breaches with time-to-breach estimate.

**Components.** 2-axis scatter, signal-source toggle list, watch list cards, predicted-breach insight cards.

**Key interactions.**
- Click radar point → "case file" for that emerging risk: contributing signals, hypothesis, recommended action, confidence, similar historical events.
- "Add to watch list" → routes to weekly digest and mover surfacing.

### 2.2 Risk / Compliance Leadership

#### Screen 4 — Control Health Universe
**Purpose.** Living visualization of the entire control population with continuous effectiveness signals. Replaces annual rating reviews with daily reality.

**Layout.**
- **Treemap (primary):** all controls grouped by Process → Risk Domain. Rectangle size = inherent risk linked; color = CES band. Visual scan reveals where control health is decaying.
- **Filter bar (top):** process, domain, control nature (Auto/Manual/ITDM), status, frequency, jurisdiction.
- **Drift watch rail (right):** controls whose CES is decaying — computed trend, not annual. Each card shows decomposition (operating rate ↓ vs. catch rate ↓ vs. evidence completeness ↓).
- **Test calendar dock (bottom):** scheduled tests + ad-hoc test queue.

**Components.** Hierarchical treemap, drift cards, test calendar, filter chips.

**Key interactions.**
- Click rectangle → control detail panel: CES decomposition over time, recent `ControlInstance`s, linked obligations, linked open issues.
- Right-click → "Test now" (routes to Reperformance Console) or "Investigate decay" (opens drift case file).
- Drag rectangle to test calendar → schedule a test cycle.

---

#### Screen 5 — Obligation Coverage Map
**Purpose.** Living obligation-to-control coverage view. Replaces the spreadsheet matrix with a visual that exposes coverage gaps at a glance.

**Layout.**
- **Force-directed bipartite graph (primary):** obligations (left cluster) connected to controls (right cluster), edge weight = `coverage_strength`. Nodes auto-cluster by regulator/process.
- **Coverage health strip (top):** % obligations fully covered, % thinly covered, % uncovered. Trend sparkline.
- **Thinly-covered rail (right):** obligations with fewer than 2 controls or with all linked controls degrading. Ranked by exposure.
- **Reg change banner (top, conditional):** "3 new regulatory events this week — see impact draft" → routes to impact assessment view.

**Components.** Force-directed graph, coverage health strip, thinly-covered cards, reg change banner.

**Key interactions.**
- Click obligation node → highlights all linked controls + shows evidence expectation in side panel.
- Click control node → reverse highlight + CES + linked process.
- "Trace mode" toggle → pick obligation, traverse forward to all affected processes.
- Click reg change banner → opens **Reg Change Impact** view (drill view, not a separate top-level screen): lineage tree from change → obligations → controls → processes, with coverage delta and recommended actions.

---

#### Screen 6 — Issue Intelligence
**Purpose.** Move issue work from triage-by-hand to triage-by-pattern. Issue clusters are computed, not authored.

**Layout.**
- **Cluster bubbles (primary):** issues clustered by computed root-cause pattern (capacity, data quality, threshold calibration, vendor delivery, resource constraint). Bubble size = open count; color = severity skew.
- **Aging strip (bottom):** issues by age bucket × severity — exposes long-tail re-aging.
- **Recommended remediation rail (right):** for selected cluster, suggested actions drawn from past closure patterns. Each shows past success rate.
- **Source filter (top):** Internal Audit / 2LoD QA / Self-Identified / Regulator / Platform-Detected.

**Components.** Cluster bubbles, aging heat strip, recommendation cards, severity histogram.

**Key interactions.**
- Click cluster → all issues in cluster + the common root-cause hypothesis + suggested action template + similar historical clusters.
- Click issue → drill to linked control / instance / evidence (the auditor's chain).
- "Apply remediation" → creates `Action`s with owners and due dates pre-populated.

---

### 2.3 Compliance Officers / Audit Managers

#### Screen 7 — Evidence Workbench
**Purpose.** End the evidence chase. Find, verify, and assemble evidence by query, not by scavenger hunt.

**Layout.**
- **Search bar (top, prominent):** natural language *and* structured. Examples: *"Show me callback evidence for wires above $100K last March,"* *"Evidence for OFAC screening on entity X."*
- **Result lattice (primary):** evidence cards arranged in a control-instance × time grid. Each card = source system badge, timestamp, hash-verification badge, evidence type icon, link to underlying step execution.
- **Lineage panel (right, slide-in):** for selected card, the trace regulation → obligation → control → instance → evidence. Each level clickable.
- **Selection tray (bottom):** workpaper-in-progress; selected evidence with running completeness check ("missing: dual-approver attestation for 2/30 instances").

**Components.** Search bar, evidence cards, lineage tree, selection tray, hash-verification badge, source-system glyph.

**Key interactions.**
- Click card → source-faithful viewer + lineage panel slides in.
- Multi-select → "Assemble workpaper" → routes to **Workpaper Studio** (final-step view embedded; not a separate screen for MVP).
- Hash badge click → tamper-detection detail.

---

#### Screen 8 — Reperformance Console
**Purpose.** Replace sampling with population testing. Turn *"did this control work?"* from a multi-week test into a query.

**Layout.**
- **Test definition (top):** control selector + auto-loaded `design_condition` (editable) + scope (date range, transaction type, jurisdiction, threshold filters). "Run population test" CTA.
- **Population view (primary, post-run):** every in-scope item plotted as a small-multiples grid or scatter. Pass = neutral; exception = highlighted; insufficient evidence = flagged distinctly.
- **Exception panel (right, slide-in):** clicking an exception shows the activity, the design condition that was breached, evidence (or its absence), and disposition history.
- **Test history (bottom):** prior runs of this test for this control, each clickable for comparison.

**Components.** Test-definition form (with rule-DSL editor for power users), population grid, exception cards, run-comparison strip.

**Key interactions.**
- "Run" → returns total tested, exception count, exception rate, with all results lineage-linked.
- Click exception → opens activity in source-faithful viewer + linked evidence in 1 click.
- "Compare to last run" → side-by-side delta.

---

#### Screen 9 — Exception Analysis
**Purpose.** Tell one-offs from patterns. Route patterns to issue creation; route one-offs to disposition. Closes the loop between detection and remediation.

**Layout.**
- **Pattern map (primary):** exceptions clustered by signature — same control, same step, same actor, same time-of-day window, same root-cause hypothesis. Each pattern = cluster with size and trend.
- **Signature timeline (below):** how each pattern is trending over the last 90 days; reveals accelerating vs. decaying patterns.
- **Promote-to-issue rail (right):** patterns the platform recommends elevating to `Issue` records, with draft issue card pre-filled (linked controls, instances, evidence, suggested owner).
- **One-offs queue (bottom):** unclustered exceptions awaiting individual disposition.

**Components.** Pattern cluster map, signature timeline, draft-issue cards, disposition queue.

**Key interactions.**
- Click pattern → all exceptions in pattern + suggested action + similar past patterns and their resolutions.
- "Promote to issue" → creates pre-populated `Issue` with all linked instances, evidence, and a suggested owner; routes to Issue Intelligence for the leadership persona.
- Disposition action on one-off → updates `ControlInstance.exception_disposition`; closes loop.

## 4. Interaction Flows

### 4.1 The cross-cutting patterns

**Two-click root cause.** Architecturally enforced. No screen ships without a tested 2-click path from primary signal to underlying empirical record. The drill stack carries context the entire way.

**Drill stack as navigation memory.** As the user drills, breadcrumb populates. Clicking earlier nodes returns without losing later filter state. Closing the drill stack returns cleanly to landing.

**Time travel cohesion.** Time scrubber recomputes the *entire* screen, including AI narrative and insight rail. State-as-of is propagated to the run-time entity log; design-time entities use their version active at that date.

**Cross-persona link preservation.** A URL to a `ControlInstance` opens the same record in any persona's frame. The auditor sees it as a test row; the CRO sees it as a contribution to RES; the Compliance Lead sees it as a CES data point.

### 4.2 Worked drill-down example: CRO → Auditor in 4 clicks

A concrete trace showing how the data model + UI + 2-click rule combine across personas.

1. **CRO opens Cockpit.** Heatmap shows Financial Crime *red* this week.
2. **Click "Financial Crime" cell** (1st click). Domain panel surfaces drivers: AML alert backlog at appetite breach + beneficiary callback completion *worsening*.
3. **Click "callback completion worsening"** (2nd click). Routes to the Control card for the wire callback control: CES decomposition shows evidence completeness dropping, with linked open issue *"Wire callback — incomplete documentation pattern."*
4. **Click "Investigate in Workbench"** (3rd click — for the auditor). Routes to Evidence Workbench, query pre-populated for callback evidence in the affected window. Result lattice shows the missing-timestamp pattern.
5. **Click any exception card** (4th click). Source-faithful viewer + lineage panel.

Each click is contextual. The CRO's first 2 clicks satisfy the 2-click rule for *understanding*; clicks 3–4 are the *handoff* to the auditor persona — same record, different frame.

### 4.3 Issue-creation flow (Exception Analysis → Issue Intelligence)

1. Auditor in Exception Analysis identifies a pattern (e.g., 6 callbacks missing timestamp/recorded number).
2. Promote-to-issue rail offers draft issue card with linked instances, evidence, suggested owner (Head of Payment Operations), suggested severity (Medium).
3. Auditor confirms → `Issue` created with full lineage.
4. Issue surfaces in Compliance Leadership's Issue Intelligence cluster ("Documentation pattern" cluster).
5. Issue contributes to RES on Cockpit; appears in Action dock for relevant owners.

One creation event, three persona surfaces, no re-keying.

## 6. AI in the UI Layer

Three integration patterns, each with explicit guardrails. AI is omnipresent but never opaque.

### 6.1 Anomaly alerts (proactive)

When the platform detects something outside expected pattern — exception-rate jump, KRI velocity outside seasonal envelope, novel process variant, unusual approver, unusual time-of-day activity — it raises an alert. Surfaces in the Cockpit insight rail, the What Changed narrative, and the Exception Analysis pattern map. Always paired with: confidence band, comparable historical events, and a suggested investigation path.

### 6.2 Insights panel (continuous)

The right rail on every screen. Ranked, persona-tuned, dismissible. Same underlying detection logic, different framing per persona — the CRO sees domain-level signals, the Compliance Lead sees control- and obligation-level signals, the auditor sees evidence- and exception-level signals. A signal seen and dismissed by one persona doesn't disappear for the others.

### 6.3 Explainability layer (mandatory)

Every AI output exposes:

- **Confidence** — calibrated probability, shown not hidden
- **Source records** — clickable list of `ControlInstance` / `KRIObservation` / `Issue` / `StepExecution` records used
- **Model identity + version** — for SR 11-7 / FCA / RBI defensibility under model risk regimes
- **Counterfactual panel** — *"what would change this conclusion"*: which inputs, if different, would flip the output
- **Drift status** — is the AI itself behaving normally? Drift on the platform's own models is a meta-control monitored on the same plane as any other control

### 6.4 Hard rules

- No AI output without lineage to underlying data.
- AI **suggests**, accountable person **decides** — every action remains human-attributable. The platform never auto-acts on 2LoD/3LoD-impacting decisions.
- The AI's own performance is a control; its drift is an issue type.
- All AI outputs honor the time scrubber — historical AI claims are reproducible from the snapshot used at that time, not regenerated against current data.
